This project aims to help teenagers (13-17) and parents (40+) learn and use AI tools like ChatGPT and Gemini easily, enjoyably, and safely.

üë• User Personas & Needs

Teens (13-17)

Needs: School assignments, creative ideas, entertainment.

Challenges: Get bored easily, prefer fast & visual content.

Approach: Casual language, visual examples, and relevant topics.

Parents (40+)

Needs: Finding information, helping their kids, communication.

Challenges: Tech anxiety, fear of making mistakes.

Approach: Step-by-step guidance, simple language.

For Teens

"Write a 200-word summary of chapter 1 of the novel 'Laskar Pelangi', using a high schooler's tone."

Example Output:

Notes:

Useful for practicing how to rephrase important information from readings in an age-appropriate style.

Prompt:

"Create 3 short and cool Instagram captions about the importance of literacy among teens."

Example Output:

"Scrolling is fine, but let's read too! 10 minutes a day can change your mind."
"All cool people... are readers. Start with you."

Perfect for digital education that's relatable to a teen's daily life.

Prompt:

"Give me a short story idea for teens about friendship and technology, set in a school."

Example Output:
An introverted student finds their true friend through a project to build a school chatbot. They form an AI Club and face challenges in an inter-school competition.
Notes:

Encourages imagination and creative ideas among teens.

Prompt:

"Explain the Proclamation of Indonesian Independence as if you were telling a story to a peer."

Makes history easier to understand and remember.

For Parents

Prompt:

"Explain what social media is to a parent who is not used to the internet, using simple language."

Example Output:
Social media is a place on the internet to share stories, photos, or videos. We can communicate with friends and family through text or pictures. Examples are Facebook and WhatsApp.
Notes:

Simple, non-technical, and suitable for beginners.

Prompt:

"Create a summary of a 5th-grade science lesson on the respiratory system, so it's easy for a child to understand."

Example Output:
The respiratory system helps us breathe. Air enters through the nose, then goes to the lungs. The lungs exchange oxygen for carbon dioxide. The dirty air is released when we exhale.
Notes:

Can be read aloud or used while helping with homework.

Prompt:

"Create a polite WhatsApp message template to offer condolences to a coworker."

Example Output:
My deepest condolences on the passing of your beloved mother. May you and your family be given strength and fortitude.
Notes:

Helps parents who lack confidence in their writing.

Prompt:

"Give 5 household budgeting tips for parents with a fixed monthly income."

Example Output:
  1. Create a monthly budget and track expenses.
  2. Prioritize essential needs.
  3. Set aside at least 10% for savings.
  4. Avoid consumer debt.
  5. Shop with a list to prevent impulse buying.
Notes:

Practical and can be immediately applied in daily life.

Prompt:

"Write a short guide for parents on how to introduce the use of AI (like ChatGPT) to a middle schooler."

Example Output:
Teach them that AI is a tool, not a replacement. Start with simple tasks, like making summaries or brainstorming ideas. Supervise its use and ensure it's used ethically. Also, discuss the pros and cons of AI.
Notes:

Boosts parents' confidence in guiding their children through new technology.

Tips for Parents

  • Ask for simple explanations: "explain in simple terms".
  • Request a step-by-step format.
  • State the purpose of use: "for a WhatsApp message".
